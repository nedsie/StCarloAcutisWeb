Advancement in Technology

Carlo Acutis is often called the ‚ÄúPatron of the Internet‚Äù because of the way he used technology for faith and evangelization. Even at a young age, he showed remarkable skill in computer programming, video editing, and website design. His greatest technological contribution was creating a digital exhibition on Eucharistic miracles around the world, which he began when he was just 11 years old. This project compiled and documented hundreds of Eucharistic miracles into a website, making them easily accessible to people globally. Carlo demonstrated that technology, when used responsibly, can be a powerful tool for good. Instead of using the internet for distraction or negative purposes, he used it as a means to spread truth, faith, and inspiration. His example shows that technology and holiness can coexist, inspiring young people to use their digital skills for innovation, education, and service to others. Education

In terms of education, Carlo Acutis showed how technology can be a tool for learning, teaching, and spreading knowledge. His digital project on Eucharistic miracles is a clear example of using technology for educational purposes, since it compiled historical, scientific, and faith-based information into a format that could be accessed by students, teachers, and ordinary people around the world. By creating an online exhibition at a young age, he demonstrated the power of digital resources in making information more engaging, interactive, and widely available.

Carlo‚Äôs life also teaches students the importance of responsible and meaningful use of technology in education. Instead of being consumed by endless entertainment, he applied his skills to something with long-term value, showing how digital literacy can be paired with creativity and purpose. His example inspires learners to see technology not just as a tool for convenience, but as a pathway for deeper knowledge, sharing ideas, and contributing positively to society.
